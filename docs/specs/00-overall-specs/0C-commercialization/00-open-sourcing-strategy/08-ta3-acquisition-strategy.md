# TA3 Partner Acquisition Strategy

## Overview

This document outlines our strategy for acquiring a TA3 health system partner for the ARPA-H ADVOCATE program, given our specific situation: strong technical capability but limited US healthcare credibility.

---

## Critical Timing: Reach Out NOW

**Key insight:** TA3 partners are ALSO submitting Solution Summaries. They need TA1/TA2 partners for their own proposals.

### TA3's Timeline Pressure

| Date | What TA3 Needs |
|------|----------------|
| **Now** | Decide which TA1/TA2 teams to partner with |
| **Feb 27** | Submit their own Solution Summary (naming TA1/TA2 partners) |
| **March** | If selected, finalize partnerships for Full Proposal |
| **April 1** | Submit Full Proposal with confirmed TA1/TA2 partners |

### When to Reach Out

| Timing | TA3 Receptiveness | Recommended Action |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **NOW (before Feb 27)** | **HIGH** - they need partners | **Active outreach** |
| After Feb 27, before selection | Low - already submitted | Wait for results |
| After selection (March) | Medium-High | Target TA3s who need stronger partners |

### Why NOW, Not After Selection

**TA3 partners are evaluating TA1/TA2 teams RIGHT NOW.**

They attended Proposers' Day specifically to find partners. They need to name partners in their Solution Summary. If we wait until after selection, many TA3s will have already committed.

### What We Say Before Selection

| TA3 Concern | Our Answer |
|-------------|------------|
| "Are you credible?" | "Read our open-source TA2 code on GitHub. Review our complete RCT protocol." |
| "Will you pass Solution Summary?" | "We're strong candidates. You can hedge by listing multiple potential partners." |
| "Why you over others?" | "Only team with open-source TA2 on GitHub. Complete RCT protocol ready for deployment." |

### Hedging Strategy

**Both sides can hedge:**
- We talk to multiple TA3s
- TA3s can list multiple potential TA1/TA2 partners
- Commitments finalize after selection, for Full Proposal

## Why TA3 Might Partner Despite Limited US Track Record

### 1. Open-Source Reduces Their Technical Risk

```
TA3's concern: "Will integration with this unknown startup be painful?"
Our answer:   "Here's the working protocol on GitHub. Test it yourself."
```

Open-source provides:
- Verifiable code they can evaluate
- Reduced integration uncertainty
- Proof we can actually build

### 2. Technical Capability Is Demonstrable

| Evidence | What It Shows |
|----------|---------------|
| 70% frontend built | We can ship patient-facing systems |
| 90% HIPAA-compliant | We understand healthcare security |
| Proprietary eval harness | We have AI/ML sophistication |
| **Complete RCT protocol** | Ready for deployment - endpoints, sample size, timeline defined |

### 3. Complete RCT Protocol Reduces TA3 Burden

**This is a major differentiator.** We're not asking TA3 to design a study - we're asking them to run one we've already designed.

| TA3 Concern | Without RCT Ready | With RCT Ready |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------|
| "How much work is this?" | High - they design study | Low - plug into existing protocol |
| "What's our commitment?" | Unclear scope | Defined endpoints, timeline, N |
| "Can we evaluate feasibility?" | Need to scope first | Review ready protocol |

### 4. ARPA-H Selection Validates

If we pass Solution Summary, TA3 partners see:
- ARPA-H reviewed us and found us credible
- Government backing reduces their risk
- Partnering with selected team is safer than unknown startup

### 5. We Bring Complementary Strengths

| We Bring | TA3 Brings |
|----------|------------|
| Technical capability (70% built) | Patient access (HF/post-MI) |
| Working TA2 stack (open-source) | Clinical expertise |
| **Complete RCT protocol** | IRB infrastructure |
| Agentic AI expertise | EHR integration |
| Rapid iteration | Study execution |

## Fit Call Strategy

### Our Pitch (From Lightning Talk Slides)

**Seeking TA3 partners** with:
- Patient Access (HF/Post-MI)
- Research Ops: Established IRB & clinical trial infrastructure
- IT Capability: Ability to provide pre-prod EHR access for integration

**Goal of fit call:** Align on governance, read-only integration plan, and study setup (so TA3 doesn't start from zero).

### Ideal Attendees

| From TA3 | Role |
|----------|------|
| Clinical Champion/PI | Medical decision maker |
| CIO/Security Lead | Technical feasibility |
| Research Ops/IRB | Study logistics |

| From Regain | Role |
|-------------|------|
| Anton Kim, PhD | CEO, technical vision |
| US Cardiologist (when recruited) | Clinical credibility |

### Messaging Guidance

**Lead with:**
1. "We've already built 70% of the TA1 frontend"
2. "We've open-sourced the complete TA2 stack - you can evaluate it now"
3. "We have a complete RCT protocol ready for deployment - you execute, not design"
4. "Our focus is making YOUR integration as low-risk as possible"

**Mention (contextualized):**
1. "We've deployed in clinical settings before"
2. "The protocol includes defined endpoints, sample size, and timeline"

**Avoid:**
1. Don't volunteer "Uzbekistan" unless asked
2. Don't cite unpublished pilot results
3. Don't oversell - let the code and protocol speak

### Key Questions for Them

1. "What's your timeline for ARPA-H decision-making?"
2. "What integration constraints do we need to work around?"
3. "Who else are you talking to for TA1/TA2?"
4. "What would make you confident in partnering with us?"
