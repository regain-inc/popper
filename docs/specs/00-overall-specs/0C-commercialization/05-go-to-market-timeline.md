# Go-to-Market Timeline: Clinical Agents System

## Executive Summary

This document outlines a 3-year go-to-market execution plan for Regain's clinical agents system. The strategy progresses from foundation (open-source, initial customers) through scale (API launch, second cartridge) to platform (marketplace, international expansion).

### Pipeline Assumptions (Bottom-Up Revenue Model)

**Definitions**:
- **ACV** (Annual Contract Value): Full annual value of a signed contract
- **ARR** (Annual Recurring Revenue): Sum of all active contract ACVs at a point in time
- **Bookings**: New ACV signed in a period
- **Recognized Revenue**: Cash recognized per GAAP (prorated for partial-year contracts)

| Segment | Leads/Quarter | Win Rate | ACV | Quarterly Bookings |
|---------|---------------|----------|-----|-------------------|
| **AI Startups** | 50 | 10% | $50K | $250K |
| **Health Systems** | 10 | 5% | $200K | $100K |
| **EHR/Platform Partners** | 2 | 25% | $500K | $250K |
| **Digital Therapeutics** | 20 | 8% | $75K | $120K |

**Assumptions**:
- Lead sources: 60% inbound (open-source, content), 40% outbound
- Sales cycle: Startups 3-4 months, Health Systems 9-12 months
- Pilot conversion: 60% of pilots convert to paid within 6 months
- Net revenue retention: 120% (expansion via additional cartridges, tiers)
- Revenue recognition: ~50% of ACV recognized in year signed (partial-year)

**Year 1 ARR Buildup**:

| Quarter | New Bookings | Expansion | Exit ARR | Recognized Revenue* |
|---------|--------------|-----------|----------|---------------------|
| Q1 | $150K | $0 | $150K | $38K |
| Q2 | $400K | $50K | $600K | $150K |
| Q3 | $700K | $100K | $1.4M | $350K |
| Q4 | $1.2M | $250K | $2.85M | $762K |
| **Y1 Total** | **$2.45M** | **$400K** | **$2.85M** | **$1.3M** |

*Recognized revenue assumes contracts signed mid-quarter; actual recognition per GAAP ASC 606. Total Y1 recognized revenue from commercial streams = $1.3M; add $2M ARPA-H grant = $3.3M total (matches revenue-streams.md).

## Distribution Channels

### Channel 1: Open-Source Community (Developer-Led)

**Mechanics**:
- Publish Hermes + Popper core to GitHub
- npm packages for easy integration
- Developer documentation + tutorials

**Funnel**:
```
GitHub stars → npm downloads → Free tier signups → Paid conversions
   1,000      →    5,000/mo   →      200         →     10-20 (5-10%)
```

### Channel 2: TRAIN Coalition Network

**Why it matters**: 16 health systems + Microsoft seeking AI safety standards

**Approach**:
1. Position Hermes as the open standard TRAIN needs
2. Offer Popper as reference implementation
3. Pilot with 2-3 TRAIN members (Cleveland Clinic, Providence)
4. Use pilots as case studies for other members

**Target**: 3-5 TRAIN members by Year 2 = $1-2M ARR

### Channel 3: Safety-as-a-Service Direct Sales

**Target**: AI healthcare startups who need FDA pathway

**Sales motion**:
1. Identify startups raising Series A/B
2. Reach out with value prop: "Ship 12 months faster"
3. Technical evaluation (2-4 weeks)
4. POC ($0, 30 days)
5. Close ($25-100K/yr)

**Sales cycle**: 3-6 months (faster than enterprise)

### Channel 4: Platform Partnerships (White-Label)

**Target**: EHR vendors, telehealth platforms

**Economics**: 15-30% revenue share

**Approach**:
1. Build Epic App Orchard integration
2. Partner with telehealth aggregators
3. White-label Popper for their AI features

### Distribution Timeline by Channel

| Quarter | Primary Channel | Target Revenue |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Q1-Q2 | Open-source community | $25K (validation) |
| Q3-Q4 | Safety-as-a-Service B2B | $200K |
| Year 2 | TRAIN + Enterprise | $2-4M |
| Year 3 | Platform partnerships | $5-10M |

### De-Risking Adoption

| Tactic | How It Works |
|--------|--------------|
| **Free tier** | Popper Community = $0 |
| **Usage-based pricing** | Pay per interaction, not upfront license |
| **Quick implementation** | Target <6 months (vs 13+ month industry avg) |
| **Contingency model** | Like SmarterDx: "Free until you see ROI" |

## Mobile App Strategy

### Recommendation: Demo App as Showcase (Phase 2)

**Prioritize API for revenue, but build demo app for credibility and investor demos.**

### Why Build a Mobile App

| Reason | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| **Proof of concept** | Demonstrates wellness mode to investors/customers |
| **B2C revenue stream** | Direct-to-consumer subscriptions ($10-50/mo) |
| **Data flywheel** | User interactions → training data → better AI |
| **Conference demos** | Tangible product to show at HIMSS, AMIA |

### Mobile App Phases

| Phase | Timeline | Purpose | Investment |
|-------|----------|---------|------------|
| **Demo MVP** | Month 6-9 | Investor demo, conference booth | $50-100K |
| **Wellness Pilot** | Month 9-12 | 500-1,000 beta users | $100-200K |
| **Production App** | Month 12-18 | B2C revenue, data collection | $200-500K |

### What the App Should Demonstrate

1. **Wellness coaching** - Lifestyle recommendations (no clinical claims)
2. **Natural conversation** - Deutsch reasoning in action
3. **Safety guardrails** - Visible Popper supervision ("Here's why I'm routing you to a clinician")
4. **Audit transparency** - "Why did the AI say this?" disclosure

### App vs. API Priority

| Factor | Mobile App | API/SDK |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Revenue potential Year 1** | $50-100K | $500K+ |
| **Enterprise credibility** | Medium | High |
| **Development cost** | $200-500K | $100-200K |
| **Sales cycle** | B2C (short) | B2B (long) |

## Summary

The 3-year go-to-market plan progresses from **foundation** (open-source, pilots) through **scale** (API, B2B) to **platform** (marketplace, international). Key success factors:

1. **Early open-source momentum** establishes Hermes as standard
2. **Pilot-to-production** motion proves clinical value
3. **Deutsch API** becomes primary revenue driver by Year 2
4. **Safety-as-a-Service** creates unique B2B opportunity
5. **Marketplace** enables platform economics by Year 3

Total revenue trajectory: $3.3M (Y1) → $12M (Y2) → $50M (Y3)
