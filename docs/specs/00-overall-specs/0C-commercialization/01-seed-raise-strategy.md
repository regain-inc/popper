## Objection Handling

### "Solo founder is a risk"

**Response:**
> "You're right — I'm actively recruiting a co-founder or COO with US healthcare operations experience. Two things reduce this risk:
> 1. The product is built. This isn't a 'can the founder code?' question — the code exists.
> 2. Advisors cover clinical (cardiologist) and regulatory (FDA consultant) expertise.
>
> The raise includes $600K-800K for team building. I expect to have a co-founder or COO within 90 days of close."

### "Uzbekistan deployments don't translate to US"

**Response:**
> "Agree — I don't lead with Uzbekistan in US market conversations. What the deployments prove is:
> 1. We can build and ship patient-facing software
> 2. We can integrate with clinical workflows
> 3. The clinical methodology works (pilot showed significance)
>
> The US opportunity requires US clinical evidence — that's what this raise funds."

### "30-36 months to FDA is too long"

**Response:**
> "Three points:
> 1. BDD accelerates the timeline — we expect 24-30 months with BDD granted.
> 2. Pre-clearance revenue is possible — wellness mode, B2B licensing, research partnerships.
> 3. Compare to alternatives — ARPA teams are 4+ years from FDA.
>
> The timeline is long, but it's certain. The regulatory path is fully mapped."

### "No US health system partner"

**Response:**
> "Active conversations with Duke, Stanford, Kaiser, and Mayo from ARPA-H Proposers' Day. We expect to confirm a TA3 partner within 90 days.
>
> Key insight: Health systems are also evaluating TA1/TA2 partnerships right now for their own ARPA proposals. We're a more attractive partner because our product is built."

### "What if ARPA-H doesn't select you?"

**Response:**
> "ARPA-H selection is additive, not required. Our strategy works either way:
>
> | Scenario | Implication |
> |----------|-------------|
> | **Selected** | +$10M funding, mandated TA3 partners, ARPA credibility |
> | **Not selected** | Execute naked speed-run, still 2-3 years ahead of ARPA teams |
>
> We submitted the Solution Summary because it's free optionality. But the raise is sized to execute without ARPA-H."

### "Wellness apps don't make money"

**Response:**
> "We're not a wellness company. Wellness mode is a:
> 1. Regulatory staging area (operate while FDA review proceeds)
> 2. Data collection opportunity (RWE for FDA submission)
> 3. User acquisition channel (convert to clinical when cleared)
>
> The real business is clinical SaaS at $500-2000/patient/year. Wellness is the on-ramp, not the destination."

### "Why not just apply to ARPA-H and wait?"

**Response:**
> "We did apply. But ARPA-H is 5 teams out of ~30-40 serious applicants. We give ourselves 22-38% odds of final selection.
>
> More importantly: waiting is strategically wrong.
> - ARPA starts July 2026, submits FDA ~2029
> - We can submit FDA ~2027 if funded now
> - First De Novo = we become the predicate
>
> The race is to FDA, not to ARPA funding. We want to be the benchmark, not benchmarked against someone else."
